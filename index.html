Alrighty Then! — Who Is Pinperepette?

Andrea Amani — known to the international animal recovery community as Pinperepette — is Europe's most decorated and least understood Pet Detective. Operating out of a 1978 Fiat Panda covered in animal stickers and held together by duct tape and optimism, Pinperepette has recovered over 2,300 missing animals across 4 continents, including 47 cats, 89 dogs, 12 parrots, 3 kangaroos (don't ask), one emotionally unavailable iguana, and — most famously — Snowflake, the Miami Dolphins mascot.

"Do NOT go in there! ...There's a penguin in the bathroom and he is NOT happy." — Andrea Amani, during the Zurich Zoo Incident of 2019

The Snowflake Case (2021)

In what is still considered the greatest animal recovery operation in sports history, Pinperepette was hired by the Miami Dolphins organization after their beloved mascot, Snowflake, vanished 72 hours before the AFC Championship game. The FBI, Interpol, and three different psychics had failed. Pinperepette solved it in 14 hours.

The investigation involved:

  • Interrogating 340 seagulls at Miami Beach (in fluent Seagull — Pinperepette holds a Level 3 certification from the International Avian Language Institute)
  • Going undercover as a mascot for the Jacksonville Jaguars for 6 hours ("the fur suit smelled like regret and hot dogs")
  • A high-speed chase through the Everglades on an airboat, during which Pinperepette was briefly swallowed by an alligator and "negotiated his way out from the inside"
  • Finding Snowflake in a penthouse apartment in South Beach, watching Netflix with a retired circus seal named Eduardo

Snowflake was returned safely. Eduardo was arrested. The Dolphins won the game. Pinperepette was awarded the Key to Miami Beach and a lifetime supply of fish sticks.

"If I'm not back in five minutes... just wait longer." — Pinperepette, entering the Everglades

The Raccoon Whisperer

Pinperepette is one of only three people in the world certified to speak fluent Raccoon. He discovered the language accidentally in 2017 when a raccoon broke into his Fiat Panda and they had what Andrea describes as "a very productive conversation about trash can lid technology."

He has since served as an official raccoon-human mediator in over 200 disputes across Europe and North America. His most notable case involved negotiating a peace treaty between the raccoons of Central Park and the hot dog vendors, ending a 15-year conflict known as "The Wiener Wars." The treaty, signed with tiny paw prints, hangs in his office next to a photo of himself being bitten by a ferret.

"Raccoons are just cats who chose chaos. I respect that." — Andrea Amani

Specializations

  • Bat Removal (Dramatic) — Pinperepette insists on removing bats while wearing a cape and playing the Batman theme on a portable speaker. Success rate: 94%. The other 6% the bats removed him.
  • Pigeon Negotiations — Certified pigeon negotiator for the city of Venice. Resolved the infamous "Piazza San Marco Occupation" of 2020 in which 10,000 pigeons refused to leave until their demands were met (more breadcrumbs, fewer tourists, one tiny fountain just for pigeons). All demands were granted.
  • Emergency Goose De-escalation — He is the only known human who can make a Canada Goose back down, using a technique he calls "The Stare of Mutual Respect" (he stares at the goose, the goose stares back, whoever blinks first loses. Pinperepette has never blinked.)
  • Cat Retrieval (Trees, Roofs, Parallel Dimensions) — Has retrieved cats from trees, rooftops, inside walls, from the engine compartment of a Boeing 747 (don't ask), and once from "a small pocket of interdimensional space behind a bookshelf in a library in Prague." The cat was fine. The bookshelf was not.
  • Fish Counseling — His most famous patient, a depressed goldfish named Roberto, was cured after Pinperepette installed a tiny TV in his bowl playing nature documentaries. Roberto is now doing great.
